Enter Space uses Apple's File Provider framework to integrate directly with the Files app on iOS and Finder on macOS. Your cloud storage appears as a location in Files/Finder, allowing you to access files with any app that supports file browsing.

Yes! Enter Space includes basic file operations like copy, move, delete, rename, and create folders directly within the app. You can also use these operations through the Files app integration.

Enter Space offers several types of automated sync operations to keep your data backed up:

  • Sync — Makes the destination match the source, copying new and changed files and deleting files from the destination that are no longer in the source.
  • Copy — Copies new and changed files from source to destination without deleting anything at the destination.
  • Bisync — Two-way sync that keeps both source and destination in sync with each other, propagating changes in both directions.
  • Move — Moves files from source to destination, removing them from the source after a successful transfer.
  • Photos Backup — Automatically backs up media from your Photos library. Choose to export photos, videos, or both, and optionally preserve metadata via sidecar files. You can export the original unedited version, the edited version, or both. Only new or changed media items are exported, so subsequent runs are fast and incremental.

Each scheduled sync task can be configured with a range of options:

  • Backup directories — Optionally configure a backup directory so that files replaced or deleted during a sync are preserved rather than permanently lost.
  • Filtering — Include or exclude files using path wildcard patterns, modification timestamps, or file sizes. Filters can be applied in an inclusive or exclusive manner, giving you precise control over exactly which files are synced.
  • Schedule options — Set syncs to run on custom intervals, or use near-realtime file monitoring to sync moments after changes are detected. They can even be triggered from Apple Shortcuts
  • Network awareness — Automatically skip syncs when on battery power, low-data networks
  • iCloud file materialization — When syncing iCloud Drive directories, Enter Space will automatically materialize (download) evicted files before the sync runs and re-evict them afterward. This lets you back up your full iCloud Documents even if the files aren't actively present on your device.
  • Duplicate tasks — Quickly duplicate an existing scheduled sync task to set up a backup to an additional location. All settings, filters, and options are copied so you can reuse your existing configuration without starting from scratch.

Enter Space also keeps detailed logs from the last 50 scheduled sync executions, so you can review exactly what happened during each run — including files transferred, errors encountered, and overall sync statistics.

Enter Space is designed so that recovering from a lost or broken Mac is as painless as possible. Most of your Enter Space settings are automatically saved to your iCloud account, and sensitive credentials like remote access tokens and passwords are securely stored in your iCloud Keychain.

The following are persisted to your iCloud account and will be available on your new device:

  • Scheduled sync tasks — All of your automated sync configurations, schedules, and filtering rules.
  • Remote configurations — Your configured cloud storage remotes and their connection settings.
  • Serve configurations — Any file serving setups you've created (e.g., WebDAV, SFTP, HTTP servers).
  • Secrets and credentials — Access tokens, passwords, and API keys are stored in iCloud Keychain for end-to-end encrypted sync across your devices.

When you sign into your iCloud account on a new Mac and install Enter Space, your previous settings will be securely restored — in most cases automatically. This means you can get back to a fully working backup setup without manually re-entering credentials or recreating your sync tasks from scratch.

The Serve feature lets you spin up a local server that exposes any of your configured remotes using a variety of Rclone serve types — including WebDAV, FTP, SFTP, HTTP, DLNA, and more. This is useful for accessing your cloud files through other apps or devices on your network.

On macOS, WebDAV and FTP serves can be automatically mounted into Finder with the Mount Helper App, giving you an alternative way to browse and manage your cloud files alongside the standard File Provider integration. This can be especially handy if you prefer a traditional mounted-volume experience or need compatibility with apps that work better with mounted drives.

*Enter Space is monitoring the features of FSKit and has plans to integrate with that framework once Apple has brought it to a maturity state ready for Network FileSystems.

While Enter Space can connect to over 100 cloud storage providers, the app runs entirely on your device. It connects directly from the app to your configured providers — Enter Space does not monitor, track, or even route your data through any intermediary servers. This means Enter Space has zero insight into how you use the app or what data you store, ensuring the utmost privacy.

Enter Space provides the client, but beyond that it is up to you to use cloud storage providers you trust. Your credentials are stored locally in your device's Keychain (and synced via iCloud Keychain if enabled), and your files travel directly between your device and your chosen provider — never through us.

Rclone is a battle-tested open-source command-line tool for managing cloud storage. It's been around for years with a proven track record of reliability and security. Enter Space is built on Rclone, giving you the power of this trusted tool with a beautiful native Apple interface.
