Why SightQuick Works

The Faster, Smarter Way to Sight In

Everything you need to dial a precise zero — and nothing you don't

🎯

Coded Printable Targets

Generate a uniquely-coded target and print it on your own paper — Letter, A4, or Tabloid — or take the PDF to a print shop for big ANSI C / ARCH D / A2 sheets. Choose a ¼-inch or 1 cm grid. A QR code embeds the exact geometry so the app knows precisely what it's looking at.

📸

On-Device Shot Detection

Photograph the target and Apple's Vision framework finds every bullet hole, straightens the page off its border, and measures each shot against your point of aim. No internet, no server — it all happens on your phone.

🔢

Exact Turret Clicks

SightQuick converts your group's offset into precise windage and elevation clicks. "Left 4, Down 3." Dial it and you're done — no MOA mental math, no ballistic charts.

📐

Two-Group Calibration

Fire a second group after dialing and SightQuick calibrates to your scope's real-world click value — accounting for the difference between a turret's marked clicks and how it actually tracks. Dead-on, every time.

⚙️

Rifle, Pistol & Bow

MOA or MIL turrets, at any distance in yards or meters — even pistol red dots at 10 yd. Shooting a bow? SightQuick measures your group and tells you which way to move your sight ("chase the arrow"). Imperial or metric, set to your region. Save a profile for every setup.

🔒

Private by Design

Your photos never leave your device. No account, no tracking, no ads. SightQuick processes everything locally and keeps your zeroing history on your phone.

Print a Coded Target

Pick your paper size and rifle profile, and SightQuick generates a uniquely-serialized ¼-inch grid target with an orange aim dot, corner registration marks, and an embedded QR code. Print it on any home or office printer.

No special paper, no targets to order and wait for. Each printout is good for one complete zeroing session you can re-photograph as you dial in.

Snap a Photo, Get Your Clicks

Fire three or more shots at the orange dot from a steady rest, then photograph the page — even at an angle. SightQuick straightens the image, finds your holes, and shows them overlaid so you can confirm or nudge any shot with a tap.

Then it tells you exactly how far to turn each turret: windage and elevation, in clicks. Dial them and fire again to confirm.

Every Rifle, Every Session

Keep a profile for each rifle and scope, and a history of every zeroing session. Pick up right where you left off, compare groups, and watch your point of impact walk into the bullseye.
