Built for the Long Shot

Eight things every Ridgeshot player learns to love.

🎯

SFP Christmas-Tree Reticle

A real second-focal-plane scope picture — etched crosshair, illuminated center dot, and a Christmas-tree subtension with yardage hashes from the close zero out to 800. Tap a hash to lock the holdover.

📐

Real Bullet Drop

Flatter than an arrow, never flat. Your shot tracks a continuous-angle ballistic model from the muzzle to impact — pick the wrong holdover and the round passes high or low exactly where the tree said it would.

💨

Wind & Patience

Live wind speed and heading on the HUD. A flag on the lane confirms it. Hold too long and your reticle starts to wander — controlled breath, clean break, or wait for the next pass. Your call.

🦌

Walking Wildlife

Olympic concentric-ring targets, or 3D whitetail, elk, turkey, and boar that wander through the scene with their own gait and pauses. Sometimes a trunk slides into your sight picture — that's the wilderness reading right.

🌲

Atmospheric Range

Alpine meadow, forest clearing, coastal bluff, desert canyon. Sunrise, noon, sunset, night. Clear, rain, snow. Every match's range is uniquely seeded — no two ridgelines twice.

💬

iMessage Challenges

Send a Ridgeshot bubble to a friend. They open it, take their shot, send it back. Each turn is one round. The whole match lives in your conversation — no app switch, no sign-in.

🎮

Game Center 1v1

Quick-match online or invite a friend. Asynchronous turn-based — your opponent shoots when they're free, you shoot when you're free. Push notifications keep you in sync without keeping you tethered.

🏆

Five Leaderboards

Total wins, longest confirmed shot, season high score, rolling accuracy, and Olympic 10-ring count. Five different ways to be measurably better than the rest. Climb whichever one suits you.

Press. Settle. Break.

Touch and hold to shoulder the rifle — the wood stock comes up, the support hand finds the forend, the scope picture zooms to a 10° FOV (the actual angular span of a long-range optic), and the reticle settles into your view.

Drag while settled to fine-tune your aim. The rifle responds with a touch of mass on purpose — you're squeezing a trigger, not nudging a cursor. Lift to break the shot.

Pick Your Holdover

Distance and target type sit on the lower-left HUD. Wind speed and direction sit on the lower-right with a rotating arrow showing exactly which way it's blowing — and a flag on the lane confirms it.

The Christmas tree gives you yardage hashes from 100 out to 800. Tap the one that matches your range, place it on the heart or the X, and let the wind decide if you need to drift a little. That last decision is the whole game.

One Bubble at a Time

Send a Ridgeshot bubble. Your friend opens it inside Messages, takes their shot from inside the bubble, and sends it back. No app to install on their end — just the iMessage extension, which downloads silently the first time someone receives a match from you.

Pass-and-play works the same way on a single device. Two shooters, one screen, alternating turns. The kind of match you can finish on a porch before the sun is fully down.
